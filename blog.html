On real numbers and why we need them

Real numbers are important. The number \(\sqrt{2}\) is irrational. Here is a proof. Suppose \(\sqrt{2}\) was rational. Then we could write it in the form \(\frac{m}{n}\) where \(m\), and \(n\) are integers with no common divisors. But this would mean that \(2n^2=m^2\) such that \(m^2\) would be even such that \(m\) would be even (note: \((2k+1)\) is odd). But this would mean \(n^2=4k^2\), implying evenness of \(n\) contradicting the assumption that the initial fraction is in its most reduced form.
